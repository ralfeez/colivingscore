⚠ Be Aware

Zoning & occupancy laws: Many jurisdictions limit the number of unrelated occupants or require permits for group housing. Verify local ordinances before purchasing or converting a property for co-living use.

Minimum room size: Bedrooms used for co-living should be a minimum of 70 sq ft with a 7 ft or higher ceiling and a legal egress window. Minimum room size standards vary by local jurisdiction — verify requirements in your area before converting or adding bedrooms.

Property condition: This analysis assumes the home is in good to excellent condition. Deferred maintenance, aging systems, or required repairs can significantly affect your real operating costs. Budget for these before committing.

Seasonal occupancy: Demand for certain tenant types — particularly students — fluctuates seasonally. Account for off-peak vacancy periods when evaluating annual cash flow projections.

Furnishing: Most co-living and midterm rental tenants expect a furnished room. Budget for furnishing costs as a one-time capital expense before your first tenant moves in.
